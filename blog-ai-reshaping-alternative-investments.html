AI is Eating Alternative Investments
— And That's a Good Thing

How agentic AI, real-time data, and fractional platforms are rewriting the rules of asset operations, loan disbursement, and investor access in India's ₹33 lakh crore alt-investment ecosystem.

MS
Manoj Sharvesh M Asset Operations · BetterInvest, Chennai

India's alternative investment landscape just crossed a tipping point. Commitments to Alternative Investment Funds surpassed ₹12.5 lakh crore in early 2025, growing at a staggering 31% CAGR. Private credit deals surged 35% in volume. Fractional real estate platforms onboarded over 100,000 investors. And beneath all of this growth, a quieter revolution is unfolding — one powered by artificial intelligence that is fundamentally reshaping how assets are originated, underwritten, disbursed, and managed.

For someone working in asset operations at the intersection of fintech and entertainment financing, this shift isn't theoretical. It's happening in our workflows every single day. The question is no longer whether AI will transform alternative investments — it's whether your operations stack is ready for what comes next.

The Old Playbook Is Breaking

Traditional asset operations relied on manual reconciliation, spreadsheet-driven disbursement tracking, and human-intensive invoice verification. In the world of invoice discounting and media asset financing — where BetterInvest operates, funding content producers against future OTT and satellite receivables — the margin for operational error is razor-thin. A delayed disbursement or misrecorded receivable doesn't just create a ledger mismatch; it erodes investor trust.

The old model scaled linearly: more deals meant more people. AI breaks that equation entirely.

Where AI Actually Hits in Asset Ops

Let's move past the buzzwords. Here's where AI is creating tangible value in alternative investment operations right now — and where the next wave will land by late 2026.

🔍

Predictive Underwriting

AI models trained on OTT viewership data, producer track records, and regional market trends can score entertainment assets before a single frame is shot — replacing gut-feel decisions with data-driven conviction.

⚡

Automated Disbursement Rails

Smart contracts and AI-triggered workflows can validate invoice authenticity, match receivables against confirmed deals, and initiate disbursements — shrinking turnaround from days to hours.

🛡️

Fraud & Anomaly Detection

With deepfake KYC and social engineering on the rise, AI-powered anomaly detection using account aggregator data is becoming non-negotiable for every fintech touching money movement.

📊

Portfolio Intelligence

Real-time dashboards powered by ML can flag concentration risk, predict default probability across asset pools, and auto-generate investor reports — making transparency effortless.

◆ ◆ ◆

The Fractional Access Revolution

Here's the story nobody is talking enough about: democratization. India now has over 17 crore demat accounts. SIP inflows have held above ₹30,000 crores monthly even during market corrections. A new generation of retail investors — many from Tier 2 and Tier 3 cities — is actively seeking yields beyond traditional mutual funds and fixed deposits.

Platforms enabling fractional ownership of commercial real estate, invoice discounting for content producers, and pre-IPO share access are making asset classes available to everyday investors that were once locked behind ₹1 crore minimums. But scaling this access responsibly requires operational infrastructure that can handle thousands of micro-transactions with the precision of institutional finance.

The premium in 2026 won't be on having AI — it'll be on having AI that delivers outcomes, not just automation. Speed, resilience, and results over tools and dashboards.

This is exactly where the role of asset operations becomes mission-critical. The ops layer isn't a back-office function anymore — it's the connective tissue between investor trust and platform scalability. Every loan disbursement, every invoice matched, every receivable tracked is a moment of truth.

What's Coming Next: The 2026 Ops Stack

Based on what we're seeing across the Indian fintech ecosystem, here's what the next-generation asset operations stack looks like:

Agentic AI will move from copilots to autonomous agents that handle end-to-end disbursement workflows, escalating to humans only on exception. Co-lending 2.0 — where banks and NBFCs partner with fintech platforms — will require API-first ops rails that can reconcile across multiple ledgers in real time. RegTech automation will become table stakes as SEBI and RBI deepen their oversight of AIF structures and digital lending platforms.

And perhaps most interestingly, AI-native compliance will emerge as a differentiator. Platforms that can demonstrate automated audit trails, real-time risk monitoring, and transparent investor reporting will win not just regulatory approvals but investor confidence — which, in the alternative investments space, is the only currency that truly compounds.

The Human Layer Still Matters

For all the power of AI, there's a dimension that algorithms can't replicate: judgment in ambiguity. When a production house delays delivery by two weeks, when a satellite deal renegotiates terms mid-cycle, when a new regulatory guideline drops — these moments demand human context, relationship awareness, and operational creativity.

The future isn't AI replacing ops professionals. It's AI amplifying them — freeing up cognitive bandwidth for the decisions that actually require experience, intuition, and stakeholder relationships. The best ops teams in 2026 won't be the biggest; they'll be the most augmented.

The Bottom Line

India's alternative investment ecosystem is at an inflection point. The platforms that combine AI-powered operations with human judgment will define the next decade of fintech.
